## What is Headless WordPress and how does it work?

:::::prose-p3
*<u><strong><a href="/services/headless-and-pwa-websites/">Headless WordPress</a></strong></u>* is a modern approach to content management that separates the presentation layer (frontend) from the content management layer (backend). In practice, this means that WordPress acts as a content management system (CMS), while the frontend of the site is handled by a separate application, most often created based on JavaScript frameworks such as Next.js (React) or Nuxt (Vue).

Such a solution allows for greater flexibility in UI design and higher performance, as the frontend can be optimized independently of the backend. What's more, thanks to the use of an API, content can be easily delivered to various devices and platforms, such as mobile applications or IoT devices.

## What are the main differences between Headless and classic WordPress?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: mb-0 [&_p]:!mb-5 [&_li]:before:!bg-transparent
---
The main differences between these two approaches concern architecture and the way of managing content:

- **1. Architecture**: Classic WordPress combines frontend and backend in one application, while Headless WordPress separates these layers, increasing security and flexibility.
- **2. Scalability:** Headless WordPress can be more scalable thanks to the possibility of optimizing the frontend independently of the backend.
- **3. Flexibility:** Thanks to the use of an API, Headless WordPress allows for easy delivery of content to various platforms and devices.

## When to choose classic WordPress, and when Headless WordPress?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-[40px] [&_ul]:!mb-10 [&_li]:before:!bg-black
---

**Classic WordPress** will work best for small and medium-sized companies, blogs and information sites that need a proven, relatively simple and economical solution. It is a good choice when the site has a standard structure, does not require advanced integrations or custom technological solutions, and at the same time is to be easy to manage by non-technical people. Classic WordPress enables quick site launch, offers a huge number of plugins and themes and provides great flexibility in further expansion.

**Headless WordPress** is worth considering for projects in which performance, scalability or integration with many communication channels are key. In this model, WordPress acts as a central content repository, and the frontend is built as an independent application (e.g., based on Nuxt).

**Such an approach offers:**

- **Full control over the frontend (visual layer) -** allows the use of modern frameworks, optimizing performance and designing custom interfaces.
- **Omnichannel -** one content base can power a website, mobile application, newsletters or other systems.
- **Higher security -** the WordPress panel can be completely invisible from the frontend level, reducing the risk of attacks.
- **Scalability and highest performance -** separating the frontend enables the use of SSR, SSG, edge rendering or custom cache layers without limitations resulting from WordPress plugins.

## Does Headless WordPress have applications in e-commerce?

:::::prose-p3
In the context of e-commerce, Headless WordPress can be an attractive option for companies that need advanced personalization and speed of operation. Thanks to the separation of the presentation layer, online stores can offer more interactive and dynamic user experiences, while simultaneously managing products and content simply in WordPress.

Thanks to such architecture, you can also easily create a frontend for many sales channels - websites, mobile applications or external systems - regardless of what CMS or e-commerce platform is on the backend.

For smaller stores that don't need such advanced features, classic WordPress with the WooCommerce extension remains a simpler and more economical solution.

## What are the costs of implementing Headless WordPress?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

Costs related to implementing Headless WordPress can be significantly higher than in the case of classic WordPress. This results from the need to hire specialists to create and maintain a separate frontend, as well as from potential expenses related to the integration of external systems.

Investment in headless architecture depends on the scope of functionalities, project architecture and requirements for the visual layer. In this type of solution, WordPress acts as a CMS, delivering content via an API, and the frontend is created as a separate modern application. This increases technical capabilities and flexibility, but also affects the scope of implementation work.

Headless can be more expensive than classic WordPress because it requires more advanced development work, but in return it offers greater control over performance, the ability to handle multiple channels and flexibility in choosing technology, which in the long run can lower infrastructure costs and facilitate development.

In summary, the choice between Headless WordPress and classic WordPress depends on many factors, such as budget, project complexity, needs for personalization and integration. Both solutions have their place on the market and can be effectively used depending on the specificity of the project and user expectations.
