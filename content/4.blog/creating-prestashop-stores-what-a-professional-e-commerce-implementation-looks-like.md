## Why is PrestaShop one of the most popular e-commerce platforms?

:::::prose-p3
PrestaShop is one of the most popular open-source platforms for online stores in Europe. The system has been developed for years with online sales in mind and offers a great deal of flexibility when it comes to expanding its functionality. A major advantage of PrestaShop is the ability to fully customize the store—both in terms of its appearance and the operation of the entire system.

The platform works particularly well for:

- medium and large online stores,
- multilingual sales,
- stores operating in multiple markets,
- extensive product catalogs,
- integration with ERP, WMS, and marketplaces,
- projects requiring custom features,
- stores focused on SEO and organic growth.

Unlike closed subscription platforms, the store owner has full control over the project's development, the code, and integration capabilities.

## Creating a PrestaShop Store - where to start?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: mb-0 [&_p]:!mb-5 [&_li]:before:!bg-black
---
One of the most common mistakes in e-commerce projects is starting the implementation without first conducting a business analysis. Simply choosing a platform does not solve sales problems. An effective online store should be based on the company’s strategy, customer needs and expectations, and clearly defined business goals. Only on this basis can you consciously plan the features, the purchasing process, and the solutions that will truly support sales growth.

**Professional PrestaShop store development should begin by determining:**

- **business objectives** - the store should be designed with specific outcomes in mind, such as increased sales, a higher number of inquiries, or sales growth in new markets. Clearly defined objectives make it easier to make the right decisions at every stage of the project,
- **sales model** - it’s important to determine early on whether the store will operate under a B2C, B2B, or hybrid model. This directly impacts the store’s features, the ordering process, and the configuration of the entire system—and, above all, the success of the entire business project,
- **target audience**—understanding the needs and expectations of future customers allows you to design a store that is intuitive, easy to navigate, and tailored to the way users make purchasing decisions
- **marketing strategy** – a well-prepared strategy answers questions such as who we’re selling to, through which channels we’ll acquire customers, and what will convince them to buy. Thanks to this, the store is designed from the outset as a tool to support the achievement of business goals, rather than becoming an ineffective solution,
- **logistics processes**—it’s worth planning in advance how orders, deliveries, inventory, and returns will be handled. This ensures the store supports the company’s day-to-day operations rather than hindering them, and it also helps you ensure a positive shopping experience for your customers,
- **planned integrations** — determining which systems the store will integrate with helps avoid costly changes in the future. This includes, among other things, integrations with ERP, CRM, payment systems, shipping carriers, and marketplaces,
- **UX and conversion expectations** - a properly designed shopping process shortens the path from landing on the site to placing an order. Intuitive navigation, a clear interface, and fast store performance have a real impact on higher conversion rates by aligning with business objectives and user expectations.

At this stage, a good agency helps organize the requirements and plan the store’s architecture in such a way that the system is ready for growth in the coming years.

## 1. Business Analysis and Project Workshops

:::::prose-p3
The first stage has a huge impact on the success of the entire project. At this point, the following are determined:

- the store’s sales model and the project’s business objectives,
- the store’s development plan and scalability,
- customer acquisition methods and marketing strategy,
- user needs and habits,
- information architecture, product search and filtering,
- the purchasing process and user experience (UX),
- sales-supporting features and integrations,
- technical requirements, SEO, and analytics.

This stage helps avoid costly changes during the development process. A well-conducted business analysis lays the foundation for making informed design decisions and sets the direction for further work on the store.

## 2. UX and UI Design for an Online Store

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

A well-designed online store makes it easier for customers to find products, shortens the path to purchase, and increases the number of completed orders. At this stage, we design solutions that combine aesthetics with functionality and support the achievement of business goals.

The UX/UI design includes, among other things:

- information architecture,
- shopping paths,
- shopping cart and checkout process design,
- product pages and product presentation,
- menu, search, and product filtering design,
- a mobile version designed with a mobile-first approach.

Every element is designed with user convenience in mind and to simplify the shopping process as much as possible. For PrestaShop stores, mobile commerce is becoming increasingly important, which is why mobile-first design is becoming the standard.

## 3. PrestaShop Implementation

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

Once the designs have been approved, the store programming phase begins. It includes, among other things:

- setting up the store environment
- configuring PrestaShop
- implementing a template,
- front-end development,
- implementing modules,
- performance optimization,
- implementing sales features.

A solid implementation is an investment in the store’s future. High-quality development translates into faster performance, security, easier scalability, and lower costs for maintenance and future updates.

## 4. Integrations with external systems

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

Integrations with external tools automate key business processes, eliminate the need to perform many tasks manually, and streamline day-to-day sales operations. The most commonly integrated tools are:

- ERP systems,
- warehouse management systems,
- courier platforms,
- online payment systems,
- marketplaces,
- CRM systems,
- marketing automation tools.

Well-planned integrations enable faster order fulfillment and fewer errors, especially as sales grow.

## 5. Technical SEO Optimization

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 mb-10 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

It’s worth taking technical SEO into account as early as the design and implementation stages of an online store. This ensures the store is better prepared for search engine optimization, and many elements won’t require costly changes after sales begin. In the context of SEO, professional PrestaShop store development includes, among other things:

- URL structure optimization,
- proper category architecture,
- store performance,
- Core Web Vitals optimization,
- structured data implementation,
- proper indexing,
- elimination of duplicate content,
- mobile version optimization.
 
By following the steps above, you’ll make it easier for search engines to index your store, increase your visibility in search results, and lay a solid foundation for further efforts to promote your store on search engines.

## A ready-made template or a custom store design?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

This is one of the most frequently asked questions during e-commerce implementations. While ready-made templates can lower startup costs, they often cause problems related to:

- performance,
- functional limitations,
- more difficult development,
- poorer UX,
- an excess of unnecessary code.

A custom store design provides significantly greater control over the user experience, the store’s performance and security, and sales optimization. For more sophisticated online stores, a custom implementation usually proves to be a more secure and cost-effective long-term solution.

## What features should a modern PrestaShop store have?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

Today’s e-commerce requires much more than a standard shopping cart. It should be tailored to the way sales are conducted, user needs, and the company’s growth. Carefully selected features streamline the shopping process, automate day-to-day tasks, and enable more effective sales growth.

Depending on business needs, a store may offer, among other things:

- advanced product filtering and search,
- multi-currency and multilingual sales
- wish lists and a product comparison tool, 
- customer reviews and ratings, including a Q&A section,
- product availability notifications,
- promotions, discount codes, and pricing rules,
- abandoned cart recovery,
- loyalty programs,
- product customization options and product configurators,
- marketplace integration,
- advanced analytics,
- marketing automation,

The most important thing, however, is that the features stem from actual business needs and support the achievement of sales goals. This ensures that the store remains easy to use, effective, and ready for growth.

## How much does it cost to set up a PrestaShop store?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

The cost of creating an online store depends primarily on the scope of the project. Factors that influence this include the number and type of subpages required, the scope and complexity of integrations and functionalities, data migration, the preparation of language and currency versions, and the complexity of the graphic design.

Simple online stores can cost as little as a few thousand zlotys, while more extensive e-commerce implementations often require a significantly larger budget.

In practice, an online store should be viewed as an investment in the company’s growth rather than a one-time expense.

## How long does it take to set up an online store?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

The time required to develop an online store, much like the cost of implementation, depends primarily on the scope of the project. Factors that influence the timeline include the complexity of the UX/UI design, the number of features, the scope of integrations, and the level of customization. The simplest stores can be built in a few weeks, but professional PrestaShop implementations with a custom design typically require several weeks of work.

The completion timeline is also influenced by the analysis phase and the efficiency of decision-making throughout the project. A well-planned process and effective communication between the client and the implementation team allow the project to proceed according to schedule, without unnecessary delays.

## The most common mistakes when creating an online store
:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

Many of the problems companies face after launching an online store stem from decisions made early in the project. These decisions result in additional costs, limited growth opportunities, and lower sales effectiveness.

The most common mistakes include:

- lack of business analysis and clearly defined goals,
- choosing solutions that are not suited to the sales model,
- designing the store solely with appearance in mind, rather than the user experience,
- failing to account for a growth plan and future business needs,
- an excessive number of modules and unnecessary features,
- a lack of integration with systems that automate business processes,
- neglecting performance and technical optimization,
- failing to consider SEO during the design and implementation phases.

A professional implementation should take into account not only the company’s current needs but also its growth over the coming years. This will ensure that the store remains competitive for a long time to come.

## Will PrestaShop be a good fit for a growing e-commerce business?
:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

Yes, especially when a store requires greater flexibility and expanded functionality. PrestaShop works well for projects that:

- plan to grow their store alongside the company’s growth,
- need flexibility and the ability to implement new features,
- handle more complex sales processes (B2B or B2C),
- want to integrate the store with the systems used within the company,
- plan to sell in multiple markets or in multiple languages,
- expect a solution that can be scaled over many years.

Thanks to its open-source architecture, the store can be developed with virtually none of the limitations typical of closed SaaS platforms.
