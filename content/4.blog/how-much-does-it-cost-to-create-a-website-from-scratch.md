Creating a website from scratch is one of the most important stages of building a brand's presence online. For companies, service providers and entrepreneurs, it is key to understand **how much a website costs** and what really affects its price. The cost of making a website can differ significantly depending on the scope of the project, technology and additional services supporting the visibility and security of the site.

## What does the cost of creating a website depend on?

:::::prose-p3
There is no single, universal price for a website. Each project is different, and the cost of implementation depends primarily on:

- type of site (business card, corporate site, online store),
- number of subpages and complexity of the structure,
- individual graphic design or use of a ready-made template,
- implemented functionalities and integrations,
- way of implementation (coding from scratch or using page builders),
- technology (most often WordPress or dedicated solutions),
- level of SEO optimization.

The cost of creating a site depends largely on the scope and quality of execution, so *<u><strong><a href="/services/websites/">website creation</a></strong></u>* should be treated as a long-term investment in a tool supporting business goals, not a one-time expense.

## Technical costs: domain and hosting

:::::prose-p3
---
class: mb-0 [&_p]:!mb-5 [&_li]:before:!bg-black
---
Every website requires a technical base. Basic costs include:

- **internet domain** – annual fee for the site address,
- **hosting** – the server where the site is stored.

Although these are relatively small expenses, choosing the right hosting is very important for site speed, security and search engine positions, so it's worth treating them as an investment in stability and further development of the service, not just as a technical cost.

## How much do different types of websites cost?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-[40px] [&_ul]:!mb-[30px] [&_li]:before:!bg-black
---

The cost of creating a website depends largely on its type and scope of functionalities. Individual types of sites differ in complexity, implementation time and scope of design and technical work, which ultimately translates into the cost of their execution:

- **business card site** - from a thousand to several thousand zlotys; a simple form of presenting a company or service,
- **corporate site** - from a few to a dozen or so thousand zlotys, depending on the number of subpages, individual project and required functions,
- **online store** - *<u><strong><a href="/services/e-commerce/">online store creation</a></strong></u>* costs from several to several dozen thousand zlotys, due to numerous integrations, automations, functionalities supporting sales and product management and security issues,
- **individual projects** - valuation set individually, tailored to specific needs and advanced solutions.

It is worth remembering that a well-designed website should be **scalable and prepared for further development**, so that in the future it is possible to easily add new functionalities without having to create the site from scratch.

## Creating a site yourself vs working with professionals

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-[40px] [&_ul]:!mb-[30px] [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-2
---
There are various website builders available on the market that allow you to create a simple site yourself without programming knowledge. Such solutions can be a good choice at the start, especially with a very limited budget or for hobby projects. However, it should be remembered that builders often involve limitations in appearance, performance, SEO optimization and possibilities for further site expansion.

For corporate sites and commercial projects, working with an experienced contractor is a much better solution. A professionally designed website is not only an aesthetic appearance, but primarily a tool supporting business goals, such as acquiring customers or building brand credibility.

**Professional websites are:**

- tailored to user needs and supporting the achievement of business goals,
- designed to build a strong and credible brand,
- designed and programmed from scratch, so they work stably for years,
- secure and ready for expansion as the company grows,
- responsive and fast, which improves the comfort of use on mobile devices,
- optimized for SEO already at the creation stage, which increases their visibility in search engines,

An additional advantage of working with professionals is technical support, advice and the possibility of further site development without having to rebuild it from scratch. An experienced contractor will prepare the service architecture at the very beginning of cooperation, based on assumptions and requirements. Thanks to this, investing in a website brings long-term benefits and allows avoiding costly mistakes at a later stage.

## Way of implementation vs cost of creation

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

One of the key factors affecting the cost of creating a website is the way it's implemented. The cheapest implementations are based on ready-made templates and page builders, which allow you to quickly launch a site at a low initial cost. This approach, however, limits project flexibility and often generates additional costs in the further development stage.

Sites built based on templates and builders contain a large number of universal functions that are not always needed in a given project. This translates into longer loading times, more plugins and dependencies that increase the risk of technical problems, security issues and make the stability of the site dependent on the quality of external solutions.

In the case of **custom web development**, the cost of producing a site is higher at the implementation stage because it includes individual graphic design and **programming the site from scratch** (especially a dedicated theme). In practice, this means that the site contains only necessary elements, thanks to which it works faster, is cheaper to maintain and expand in the long run, and above all works stably for years.

The difference in price between a template-based site and a site created from scratch is large, but it results mainly from the amount of work and quality of execution. Ready-made solutions lower the starting cost, while custom development minimizes technical costs and the risk of rebuilding the site in the future, which in the long run often turns out to be more profitable.

## Additional services affecting the site price

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

The ultimate cost of a website can increase depending on the scope of additional services that have a direct impact on its effectiveness, security and visibility on the internet. The most frequently chosen include:

- **copywriting**, tailored to both users and search engine algorithms,
- **integration with analytical tools**, enabling monitoring of traffic and user behavior,
- **implementation of automations**, enabling, among others, the creation of extensive marketing campaigns,
- **SEO optimization** (technical and content), which helps the site achieve better positions in search results,
- **technical care** after site launch, including updates, backups and ongoing support.

Although these services increase the initial cost of implementation, in practice they often decide whether the website really supports acquiring customers and business development, or remains only an aesthetic business card in the network.

## Website maintenance costs

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-8 [&_li]:before:!bg-black [&_li]:!mb-1 [&_ul]:!mb-8
---

After launching a website, fixed costs related to its ongoing operation should also be taken into account. These are expenses that ensure stability, security and correct functioning of the service in the long term. The most important include:

- **domain and hosting renewal**, necessary to maintain site continuity,
- **system and plugin updates**, which protect the site from security gaps and technical problems,
- **backups and security monitoring**, enabling quick site restoration in case of failure,
- **ongoing *<u><a href="/services/support-and-development/">technical support</a></u>***, allowing response to problems and making minor changes,
- **functionality development and UX optimizations**, in response to changing business needs and user habits.

Regular website maintenance avoids costly failures, increases data security and ensures its stable and reliable operation on a daily basis.

## How to choose a website contractor?

:::::prose-p3
---
class: ml-0 [&_p]:!mb-[20px]
---

When choosing a website contractor, it's worth paying attention to several key aspects that will allow assessing their experience and quality of services. Above all, the portfolio and previous projects are important because they show the style of work and technical level of the projects. Equally important are customer reviews, the range of services offered and clear, transparent cooperation and valuation terms, which allow avoiding misunderstandings at a later stage.

An experienced contractor is not only a person or company that will design a website, but also a partner advising on technology, functionalities and further development of the service. Such an approach allows creating a site tailored to real business needs and prepared for future changes.

The cost of creating a website from scratch depends on many factors: from the type of site, through functionalities, to additional services and technical care. A well-planned budget and conscious technological decisions allow creating a site that will be real support for the business.
